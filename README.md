# TCB Smart Family Card Project (Our findings & Solutions)

## Main Context

## What is TCB Smart Family Card ?

![Family Card](https://dscdn.daily-sun.com/english/uploads/news_photos/2024/11/18/1731945736-0d0adb12ef27f16c2795911db3686df2.jpg)

The Trading Corporation of Bangladesh (TCB) family card is a subsidized food initiative supporting low-income households with essential commodities like rice, lentils, oil, and sugar at reduced prices. As of late 2025, the program is transitioning to 10 million smart cards—replacing old paper cards—to curb corruption, following the cancellation of 4.3 million fraudulent card

### Key Points

![Family Card](https://outspoken.newagebd.com/files/img/202411/54bd1ced469b6c287067d6f7d90c1eda.jpg)

- Objective: To provide relief to low-income families by selling essential items below market rates, mitigating price shocks.

- Products & Quantity: Cardholders can typically purchase up to two litres of edible oil, two kg of lentils, one kg of sugar, and five kg of rice monthly. During Ramadan, items like dates and chickpeas are added.
- The Shift to Smart Cards: Following verification, 4.3 million fraudulent, duplicate, or ineligible cards were cancelled, leaving 5.7 million legitimate recipients as of early 2025. New smart cards with QR codes are being issued to prevent further misuse.

- Target Audience: Targeted at 10 million low-income, poor, or vulnerable families, with beneficiaries selected by local administrations and representatives.
- Distribution: Goods are sold through designated TCB dealers across the country.
- Cost: The package is designed to be affordable, often costing around Tk 540 for goods with a market value closer to Tk 1000, allowing for a significant monthly subsidy.

## Current Situation of this initiative

- Active Distribution & Usage: Regular monthly sales for TCB family cardholders are ongoing, with more than 66 lakh active family cards currently in circulation.
- Transition to Smart Cards: TCB is in the process of converting traditional hand-written cards into smart family cards featuring QR codes to prevent duplication and ensure transparency. About 57 lakh cards have been prepared for this, with efforts to eliminate previous irregularities.

## Technical issues that we are pointing

- Accountability: A beneficiaries can't track there monthly purchase of essential goods through any platform. Sometimes a beneficiaries can make 2 or more family card with using same NID and can purchase more goods illigally.

- Smart Card Conversion & NID Integration: The government is in the process of converting roughly 10 million (1 crore) manual cards into smart cards. This process involves verifying beneficiary data against the National ID database to eliminate fraudulent or duplicate entries.

- Massive Card Cancellation: Due to technical verification with NID systems, approximately 4.3 million (43 lakh) TCB cards were previously identified as having anomalies and were slated for cancellation or revision.

- Verification Status: As of November 2024, around 5.7 million (57 lakh) smart cards had been prepared, while the remaining cards were still undergoing verification by local administrations which are very lengthy processes.

- Temporary Coexistence of Systems: Despite the push for modernization, TCB is currently operating a mixed system where both new smart cards and, in some cases, older manual cards are used, causing some logistical complications.

- Digital Access: There is a "TCB Smart Family Card Sheba" Android application available for users to manage their smart card services. But this app doesn't work. No key support in this app

## Problems & Corruption Highlight

- Delays in Smart Card Distribution: Thousands of families have faced long delays in receiving their new smart family cards. For instance, in Barishal City Corporation, approximately 28% of applicants (over 25,000 families) had not received their smart cards, despite repeated visits to local offices.

- Card Cancellation and Data Inconsistencies: Around 4.3 million TCB family cards were canceled nationwide due to inconsistencies found when verifying data against the National ID (NID) system during the transition to smart cards.

- Corruption and Irregularities: The enrollment and distribution processes have been plagued by irregularities, including demands for bribes (Tk 50-200) from beneficiaries to receive their cards. There are reports of nepotism, where cards were issued to ineligible individuals, including relatives of local representatives, rather than those in need.

- Logistical Challenges at Sales Points: Beneficiaries often face long waiting times, sometimes up to 10 hours, to purchase products. Some sales points lack adequate, separate lines for women and disabled individuals.
